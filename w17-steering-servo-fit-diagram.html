Steering — the DS3235SG mounts side-on

From Codex's mechanical fit study (2026-07-22): keep the DS3235SG, lay it on its side (~20 mm tall, shaft lateral). An ordinary 25T metal horn — vertical at neutral — pushes a short M4 tie rod to the adjacent front saver, where servosaverv7 pivots on an M3 boss. Shaft-up was never the real orientation.

What this corrects from the first version

  • Side-on does steer. "a lateral shaft can't drive steering" — the horn's fore/aft tangent drives a short M4 tie rod; the front saver/bellcrank converts it. No bevel gear.
  • servosaverv7 is a front M3-pivot part, not a servo saver. "it presses onto the 25T spline" — its bore is 2.90 mm (M3). The servo uses the ordinary aluminium 25T horn (the one in your first photo — it is used).
  • Keep the DS3235SG. shaft-down / floor-well / low-profile swap — all rejected; side-on removes the height problem.

Assembly / fit-test order

  1. Print & stand the holder — 58 mm span longitudinal, 22.89 mm axis vertical.
  2. Slide the servo in side-on, power off — shaft horizontal/lateral, output-boss end toward the rod route. Don't force or grind the case; record contact + shell clearance.
  3. Centre the servo (existing servo-test procedure — no firmware change; still GPIO 13 · 1500 µs).
  4. Fit the 25T horn vertical at neutral — start at the 19.5 mm hole (closest to the front-link height).
  5. Build the oil-shock front and mount servosaverv7 on its M3 pivot boss.
  6. Connect the M4 servo tie rods (≈22 mm) — spacers for a free, near-level run; don't bend them into plane.
  7. Sweep L/R at ride height + bump; log rod Z/L and any rub. Closes the physical half of D-26.
  8. Seat the shell before nearby electronics; require ≥5 mm static + ≥8 mm to moving rod/joint.

The one open risk + fallback

If it won't seat without force (Codex option E): a minimally-relieved original-style holder — keep the 58 × 10 footprint + 42 mm span, raise the bridge to measured case width + 0.4 + 4.39 mm (≈24.79 mm outer for a true 20 mm case). Recheck ≥5 mm shell clearance before printing. No STL cut yet — it waits on your measured case.
Only if the servo itself must change: a low-profile 25T unit with case width ≤18.5 mm for the stock holder. Closest documented is the AGFRC SA33 (40.8 × 20.2 × 29.5, 25T Ø5.92, 25 kg·cm @6 V) — but its 20.2 mm width still needs the relieved holder, and its ear pitch / stall current are purchase gates. Not a verified drop-in.
